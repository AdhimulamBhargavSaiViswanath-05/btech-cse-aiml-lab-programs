An Information Retrieval IR system is software designed to find and retrieve relevant information from a large collection of unstructured data, like web pages or documents, based on a user's query. Its primary goal is not just to find items that contain specific keywords but to understand the user's intent and rank the results by relevance, so the most useful information appears first. Unlike a simple database that finds exact matches in organized fields, an IR system works with messy, free-form text by first indexing the content to make it searchable and then using algorithms to score and sort the results. You interact with these systems daily through web search engines like Google, the search bar on a shopping website, or the search function in your email.
