Modern computer graphics integrates high-level mathematics, computational algorithms, and artistic principles to produce complex visual content for applications including film, video games, virtual reality, scientific visualization, and simulation training. Core topics involve 3D modeling techniques such as spline-based surfaces, polygonal meshes, voxel grids, and procedural generation, combined with rendering algorithms that utilize global illumination, ray tracing, path tracing, and real-time shading. Advanced simulations incorporate rigid-body dynamics, fluid mechanics, soft-body deformation, and particle systems to achieve lifelike motion, while GPU programming, shader development, and parallel processing optimize performance and enable real-time interactivity. Material and texture representation, lighting models, and post-processing effects are analyzed to enhance perceptual quality, including the simulation of subsurface scattering, reflections, refractions, and volumetric effects. Emerging AI-driven methods, including neural rendering, style transfer, and procedural content generation, introduce new challenges in integrating automated outputs with traditional pipelines while maintaining artistic control and computational efficiency. Discussions also explore cross-platform compatibility, performance optimization, algorithmic trade-offs, and usability, emphasizing both the technical and creative aspects of producing high-fidelity visual content. This multidisciplinary approach bridges the gap between science, engineering, and art, requiring practitioners to understand theoretical foundations while maintaining practical proficiency in software and hardware constraints.
